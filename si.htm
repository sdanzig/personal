Split Infinity is a project I started October 17th, 1996 as a hobby during college.  It's classified as a MUD (Multi-User Dimension) which in layman's terms can be described as this:

 

A place on the internet where lots of people can connect to and chat with each other. (Much like IRC, a MUD is fully text-based as well ... just words, no graphics.)  In addition to the name you choose that other people see you as, you design an entire character for yourself.  Upon login, you write up a description for yourself, choose your race, your statistics (strength, intelligence, charisma, etc.) and finally log in as the character you created.  You have an inventory with some neat stuff such as weapons, food, armor, a map, a torch, and other adventuring supplies and strange baubles and artifacts you gather during your travels.  That's right, you can travel too.  You start out in a room that has a description you can read.  There might be other things in this room that you can look at or do other things with.

 

There's a few "tutorial rooms" that new characters are forced to go through to give you a feel for the MUD.  Once done with the tutorial, you can move from room to room, adventuring around the hundreds upon hundreds of rooms to explore, solving quests, finding treasures, fighting off a variety of foes, some controlled by other people currently connected.  You are not left to fend for yourself, however.  There are lots of other people connected to Split Infinity that are willing to help you when you need it.  Also, the population of Split Infinity is diverse.  The coding team that helps develop this project  include people from not only all over the United States but also from countries such as Sweden, Germany, Denmark, Australia, Holland, England, South Africa, Canada and Mexico.  View the official web site at www.si.nu and then when you want to see what Split Infinity is truly like, go ahead and: telnet si.nu 4000
