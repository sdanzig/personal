Blue Quartz

As the trees entrench the air in summer's sensations,
The golden sun rises over the dew-dripping leaves,
And the slight waves of water swirl around the slickened stones,
In slender creeks which curl around the glistening glades.

And the leaves leave the trees in vibrant veils,
Of orioles that flutter, canaries that sing,
And cardinals that frolick and fall,
Sheathing the autumn earth in their fiery splendor.

A sun-lit rose sways over the snowy swards,
And light pours down through its sanguine petals,
Onto the angelic alabaster ground, gracefully blushing,
Smoothed by a blanket of gentle fervor.

Ducks float past dazzling flashes from heaven's mirror,
Bordered by lazy spring lilies and lichened logs,
Speckled luxuriously with the sunken sparkles,
From the crystalline treasures beneath its glass.
