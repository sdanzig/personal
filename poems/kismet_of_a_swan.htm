Kismet of a Swan

A slender swan's devotion lights her eyes,
With timeless patience marked by countless moons,
And longing sighs begot by daydreamt swoons,
Yet kindless kismet shed its pure disguise.

So fair a fowl doth deserveth love,
Her feathers quite refined and glist'ning white,
Her eyes with passion, mind so erudite,
And frisky flut'ring wings to soar above.

Although the swan's foretelling's now unknown,
The world's a medley ev'ry marv'lous day,
Where dreamers stray, romantic hearts run 'way,
Where gorgeous flowers grow where weeds are hewn.

When sunsets dim, the pond seems bleak,
But furry ducklings reach to nuzzle'er beak.
