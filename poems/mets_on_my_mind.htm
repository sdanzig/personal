Mets on my Mind

The Mets on my mind,
Daydreaming of the day when 'eros emerged.
A lineup to love,
A rotation to revere.

A mound with muskets of marksmen,
Firing balls elusive as an escaping mouse,
Which prance like pebbles in a rockslide.
Batters sedated by darts from Pedro's blowgun.

Fly balls turn to breadcrumbs,
In a field freckled by a flock of sparrows.
With Doug wielding the glove of La Brea,
Ground balls are slain by knights guarding their king.

And the knights charge the bases...

Like litter in a hurricane,
Ricocheting off bases like bent lasers.
Jose becomes a hungry fox,
Hunting the hare at home.

The strongest champions accept their challenge.
Carlos, composing a constellation of frozen ropes.
Cliff, sending a warning shot to Icarus.
Mike Piazza, harpooning a god.

Enthralled by enthusiasm,
As each battle begins.
The Mets on my mind,
The days have been kind.
