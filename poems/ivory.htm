The Ivory Tower

The ivory tower oversees the sky,
The stars concede to the fabulous sun,
Rolling green hills tingle giddily,
Caressed by light in a sparkling run.

Near the earth, mortality shows,
To unbidden ivy, life rescinds.
Beauty scorched, malevolently,
Chafed by the unbridled winds.

Dawn glimmers on the ivory dream,
Tragically, the fantasy crumbles.
Such angelic innocence condemned,
Utopia, so sweet, so lethally humbled.

A mirthful child wails, in tears,
A singing canary ends its song.
Why must it end this way?
Is it not vilely wrong?

The ivory spirit floats ruefully away,
Bestowing wondrous peace no more.
An empty land left behind,
Save the sickness we dodged before.
