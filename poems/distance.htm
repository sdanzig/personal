Distance

O'er the roaming land beneath me to sea,
The petty squabbles and baubles so small,
Imagine! Yet taller I stand, not tall
At all. Lowly I am, privileged I be,
To share with the sparrow its ecstasy.
How eyes can soar as they answer the call,
Whisked by the brisk wind unable to fall.
Carry my heartache high upwards with thee!
Send it afar, so high and far away,
A mere speckle in civilized rubble,
Engulfed in hunger that distance decays.
Yet I squint in desperation, "Allay
My forged mind, and alleviate the trouble,"
But to them I'm a distant, squabbling stray.
