Steadfast Carapace

So lost, confused, the scary city's night,
Where lofty silhouettes can scrape the sky,
And bustling feet and screeching tires fly,
The tortoise finding home a hopeless plight.

The wand'ring soul meanders toward a field,
Deserted, desolate, depressing land,
The sunshine hotter than the tortoise planned,
Through whipping winds its weary legs won't yield.

Increasing fear as hopeful dreams erode,
And panicked visions plague relenting not.
A shell--that heavy burden's all its got,
But there and then it finds its safe abode.

The tortoise burrows down while counting sheep,
Then curls up tight while falling fast asleep.
