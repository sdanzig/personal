Ode to a Nutcase

Poems wisk around in a stir of the tongue.
With chin on cheek students listen and hear,
The words of estrangement that rage and swear.
Fidgeting and frowning, our thoughts among
An intrusive but happy reaching begun.
Like toddlers we rise embarrassed and near,
Displacement desired, helplessly steered,
For purposeless amusement whirled and flung.
A self-conscious conscience will supercede
Instruction of the imperfect pupil.
Pundit thoughts which a capricious mind seeds
Will grow strides to strife and leave them tranquil.
Beneath the brow, a crazed gaze in the eyes,
Brings forth fear, of a crazed man in disguise.
