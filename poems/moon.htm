The Moon

In the midnight sky, heaven's pale moon rests,
With the warmest glow amongst starlight, high
Aloft, aflight, shy, in its slender crest,
As dreary, dark wisps veil the sight espied.
Over sealed-shut stares, it serenades souls,
Spirits seduced into celestial cells.
Alas, Selene's secretive dreams are stol'n,
Solving sorrows with serenely sung spells.
Silken beams slip through fine rends in darkness,
Seams of mystery stitched by threads of truth,
Inspiration blankets minds of dullness,
And unsettled hearts its chilled grace will sooth.
Sleep in the fortune of your moonlit gift,
Let a smile touch your lips, worries adrift.
