Djej Matisha Mesla

Marinade:

  • 1 clove of garlic
  • ½ tsp ginger powder
  • ½ tsp saffron powder
  • ground black pepper
  • 2 tbsp olive oil

Dish:

  • 225 g (0.6 lb) chicken breast
  • 1 onion
  • 100 ml (0.42 c) water
  • 400 g (1.07 lb) beef tomatoes
  • 1–1½ tbsp cinnamon
  • 2¼ tsp (also called a "small tablespoon") honey
  • salt
  • 10 g roasted sesame seeds (optional)

Marinade:
Chop up the garlic and mix it with the rest of the herbs. Cut the chicken into medium-sized cubes and mix it in thoroughly. Let it stand for a few hours, stirring occasionally.

Dish:
Grate the onion or chop it up very finely. Put water, chicken, marinade and onion in a large pan, bring it to boil and let it simmer for 15 to 20 minutes. Remove the skin of the tomatoes. This can be done by cutting a shallow cross into the bottom of each tomato, then dunk them in boiling water for 20 seconds, then again in cold water. The skin should peel right off. Next, remove the seeds and "hard" interior bits from the tomatoes and cut them into small pieces. Add the tomatoes and half of the cinnamon to the chicken mixture. Add salt to taste. Let the dish simmer for another 20 to 25 minutes. Remove the chicken. Boil down the sauce until it takes on a nice, thick consistency. Add the honey. Mix in thoroughly. Add the chicken and heat it through and through. Sprinkle on the cinnamon just before serving. Serve warm. As an option: sprinkle sesame seeds over the sauce.

Serve with rice or couscous and cold corn (from a can).
