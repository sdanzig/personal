Lamb-Haricots

  • 1 lb assorted dried beans, such as Great Northern, flageolet, cranberry, and cannellini, picked over and rinsed.
  • 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • one 3 lb boneless lamb shoulder, cut into 6 pieces
  • salt and freshly ground white pepper
  • 6 cloves garlic, peeled and split apart
  • 2 stalks celery, peeled, trimmed, and cut into 1 inch lengths
  • 1 onion, peeled, trimmed, and cut into 1 inch chunks
  • 1 fennel bulb, trimmed and cut lengthwise into quarters
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 5 large tomatoes, peeled, seeded, and finely chopped
  • 2 c dry white wine
  • herb sachet, made by taking 10 sprigs of Italian parsley, 6 sprigs thyme, 2 sprigs rosemary, 2 bay leaves, 1 branch fennel--reserved from the fennel bulb--and 1 teaspoon black peppercorns, all bagged and tied together with cheesecloth and twine.
  • 8 cups unsalted chicken stock.  This can be made as directed below, or you can just buy low-sodium chicken broth from the store. You probably would want to store the remaining chicken stock for future use.  You can store it at least a month in the freezer, as chicken broth often isn't made just for one use.

Ingredients for homemade chicken stock:

  • 4 lb chicken necks, backs, wings, or other chicken parts, skinned, fat trimmed, and rinsed
  • 2½ gallons cold water (never use hot tap water)
  • 2 medium onions, peeled, trimmed, and cut into quarters
  • 2 small carrots, peeled, trimmed, and cut into 2 inch pieces
  • 1 stalk celery, trimmed and cut into 2 inch pieces
  • 1 medium leek, trimmed, split lengthwise, and washed
  • ½ head garlic, split crosswise in half
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 5 sprigs Italian parsley, washed
  • 1 tsp white peppercorns

Procedure for making homemade chicken stock:
Put the chicken and 7 quarts of the cold water in a tall stockpot and bring to a rolling boil. Add the remaining 3 quarts water (it should be very cold) and skim off the fat that rises to the top. Adjust the heat so that the water simmers and let simmer for 10 minutes, skimming regularly.  Add the remaining ingredients to the pot and simmer for three hours, continuing to skim so that the stock will be clear. Drain the stock in a colander. Allow the solids to drain for a few minutes before discarding them, then strain the stock through a fine mesh strainer. Let cool, then refrigerate.

Preparing the beans:
Put the beans in a large pot and cover them with about three times their volume of cold water; allow the beans to soak overnight in the refrigerator. Drain and rinse the beans afterwards.

Cooking the lamb:
Put a rack in the center of the oven and preheat to 325 F.

Warm the oil in a large skillet over medium heat. While it is heating, season the lamb all over with salt and pepper. Slip the lamb into the pan and brown the parts very well on all sides. When the lamb is deeply colored, transfer it to a large casserole dish and set aside. Pour off all but 2 to 3 tablespoons of the fat in the pan. Add the garlic, celery, onion, carrot, and fennel, season with salt and pepper, and cook, stirring, until the vegetables are translucent but not colored, about 10 minutes. Add the tomato paste and tomatoes and cook, stirring, until the tomatoes soften. Add the wine and simmer for about 5 minutes, to burn off the alcohol and reduce the liquid by half. Transfer everything from the pan to the casserole dish and stire in the beans, herb sachet, and chicken stock.

Put the casserole dish over medium heat and bring the liquid to a simmer. Cut a sheet of parchment paper to fit inside the dish and cover the dish with the parchment paper lid and then put the casserole dish's own lid over that. Slide the dish into the oven and leave undisturbed for 2½ hours, or until the meat is tender enough to be cut with a spoon. Check around 2 hours into cooking. If it seems too dry, you can stir in up to 1 cup of water as it's cooking, as it should be kept moist and saucy. Discard the herb sachet and taste the beans; add more salt or pepper if needed.

Serve from the casserole dish, topping a generous portion of the beans with a piece of the lamb.
