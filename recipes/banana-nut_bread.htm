Banana-Nut Bread

  • 1¼ c sugar
  • ½ c butter (1 stick), softened
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1½ c mashed very ripe bananas (Usually 3 large bananas will suffice. Make sure the bananas are covered with brown spots with a pungent banana scent.)
  • ½ c buttermilk (powdered buttermilk mix will work just fine)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2½ c all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 c coarsely chopped pecans

Move oven rack to low position so that the tops of pans will be in the center of the oven. Heat oven to 350 F. Grease bottom only (I have forgotten before to only grease the bottom, so here's another reminder) of a 9 x 5 x 3 inch loaf pan with vegetable shortening.

Mix sugar and butter in a large bowl. Knead the butter with a spoon until the sugar and butter is of an even consistency and thoroughly mixed. Stir in eggs until well blended. Stir in bananas, buttermilk, and vanilla; beat until smooth. Stir in flour, baking soda and salt just until moistened. Stir in nuts. Pour batter evenly into pan.

Bake for about an hour then start checking every 5 minutes to see if a toothpick inserted into the center of the loaf comes out clean. Since this is a fairly thick loaf, you should pull the loaf out of the oven the moment it's ready. Otherwise, the outside might start to get overcooked and you might end up scraping off charred edges. Cool 10 minutes in pan on a wire rack then loosen sides of loaves with a butter knife and gently shake out of the pan, then wrap up tightly with plastic wrap and store in the refrigerator for 24 hours. This refrigeration helps strengthen the bread, so it won't crumble when you slice it. As a side note, it's also worth heating up a slice or two in the microwave (just until warm, no longer) and trying it that way.
