Blueberry Sour Cream Coffee Cake

  • ½ c (1 stick) butter
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 c sugar
  • 1 c sour cream
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 c flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 2 c fresh blueberries, floured (harden them by freezing them overnight)
  • ½ c light brown sugar
  • ½ c chopped walnuts
  • ½ tsp cinnamon
  • powdered sugar

Cream butter and sugar until smooth. Blend in eggs, sour cream, and vanilla. Stir in flour, baking soda, and salt, and beat until smooth. Gently fold in blueberries. Spread half batter in greased 13 by 9 inch pan. In separate bowl, stir together brown sugar, nuts, and cinnamon. Sprinkle evenly over batter in the pan. Spread the remaining batter on top, carefully. To spread without making a mess, drop the batter as evenly as possible over the nut mixture. Take a rubber spatula/scraper and spread the batter gently over the mixture, starting with the outer edges first, getting the batter to touch the side of the pan and adhere to it. The trick is to not lift the spatula. Rotate the pan as you spread the batter, spiraling to the center.

Bake at 350 F for 40 minutes, either until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean, or until the edges start getting dark brown. Allow to cool for 10 minutes, then dust with powdered sugar.
