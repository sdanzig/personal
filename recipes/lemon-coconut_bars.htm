Lemon-Coconut Bars

  • 1 c all-purpose flour
  • ½ c softened butter.  Just leave a stick of butter out until it's soft and can be easily smeared.
  • ¼ c powdered sugar (confectioner's sugar).  Keep the rest of the bag handy too, for decoration.
  • 1 c granulated sugar
  • 2 tsp freshly grated lemon peel. Don't grate the white stuff, just scrape off the yellow part.
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice.  You can squeeze the same lemon for this.
  • ½ c flaked coconut.  You could get away with using the sweetened coconut flakes you get in the store, but I find cracking open and coarsely grating a fresh coconut is fun and gives a better result. Most won't notice the difference though.
  • ½ tsp baking powder
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 2 large eggs

Heat oven to 350 F

Mix flour, butter, and ¼ cup powdered sugar. Press in ungreased square pan, around 8 x 8 x 2 inches, building up ½-inch edges.

Bake crust 15 minutes; remove from oven once parts of the crust look a bit gold/brown. If you're not sure, just take it out. Undercooking can be remedied.

While baking, beat remaining ingredients except the powdered sugar with an electric mixer on high speed for about 4 minutes. Pour over the hot crust when it's ready.

Bake around 25 minutes longer. It's done when you can tap your finger very lightly on the center and there's no indentation. If the edges start to get anywhere near dark brown though, you've probably misjudged and it's ready immediately. Cool in the pan placed on a wire rack or something close to one. Sprinkle very stingily with powdered sugar from about a foot over the pan and cut into 5 rows, 5 columns and serve ASAP (hot is better, although it's still delicious cooled off too).
