Almond Crunch Pumpkin Cheesecake

Crust:

  • 2 c graham cracker crumbs
  • ⅓ c + 1 tbsp sugar
  • ¼ c sliced almonds, chopped
  • 5 tbsp melted margarine

Filling:

  • 3 packages (8 oz each) softened cream cheese
  • ⅔ c sugar
  • 4 eggs
  • ½ c dairy sour cream
  • 1 c solid pack canned pumpkin
  • 2 tsp pumpkin pie spice (should be available with the fancier spices in any grocery store)

Almond crunch topping

  • 3 tbsp margarine
  • ¼ c firmly packed light brown sugar
  • ½ c sliced almonds
  • ½ c flaked coconut (real grated coconut is better, but the packaged sweetened coconut is okay, just leave out 2 teaspoons of the sugar)

Prepare the crust: In bowl, mix graham cracker crumbs, ⅓ cup + 1 tablespoon sugar, chopped almonds and margarine; press on bottom and 2 inches up side of 10 inch spring form pan; set aside.

Prepare the almond crunch topping: In small saucepan, heat margarine and brown sugar until dissolved. Stir in sliced almonds and flaked coconut.

In large bowl, with electric mixer at medium speed, beat cream cheese and remaining ⅔ cup sugar until smooth; blend in eggs, sour cream, pumpkin and spice. Pour into prepared crust. Bake at 350 F for 1 hour. 50 minutes into the baking, when just barely firm enough, gently spread on the almond crunch topping, then continue baking for the remaining 10 minutes. This interruption allows the almond crunch topping to stick to the top of the cheesecake more securely. After the hour is up, turn off the oven.

Open door slightly and leave cheesecake in oven for another 30 minutes. Put cheesecake in broiler, a few inches under the heat source, just to fully cook the topping, turning it golden brown. Chill overnight before serving.
