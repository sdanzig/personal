Bazin-quiche

Crust:

  • 2 c flour
  • dash of salt
  • 1 c (2 sticks) butter
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • At least 2 tbsp cold water

Filling:

  • 4 eggs
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¾ lb ground beef
  • 4 strips of bacon
  • 6 diced medium-sized mushrooms
  • ½ medium onion, chopped up
  • 1½ c grated sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1½ c milk
  • 2 tbsp flour
  • paprika

Preheat oven to 350 F.

To make the crust:
Mix flour and salt in bowl. Cut in 1 cup butter and oil until mixture resembles coarse meal (tiny pieces, but much coarser than sand). Sprinkle with water, tossing with fork until dough holds together, sprinkling a bit more water if necessary. Gather into ball and chill a few minutes if   dough is too soft to handle. Roll out pastry on floured board and fit into 10 inch spring-form pan, forming a ¾ inch edge.  Leave in oven for about 3-4 minutes to dry out the remove and set aside.

To make filling:
Cook ground beef in a frying pan on medium heat, chopping and stirring until brown. Let drain on paper towels while patting down VERY thoroughly. Press down on the beef to extract juice from it. You want it to be as dry as possible. Set aside.

Pour off fat from beef and cook bacon in the pan next, on high heat, frying until crispy. Move to paper towels and pat down just as thoroughly, then crumple the strips. Set aside.

Cook the mushrooms and onion in the pan over medium heat, frying until tender. Move the mushroom and onion onto a paper towel to drain and once again pat down to dry thoroughly, squeezing some of the juice out.

Mix eggs, milk, and flour in a bowl and beat well till fully blended.

Spread cheese evenly in bottom of crust.

Mix in a separate bowl the beef, mushrooms and onion, crumbling the bacon strips and mixing those in as well, then spread the mixture evenly over the cheese.

Slowly pour in the egg mixture.

Pour in egg mixture.

Bake at 375 degrees 35 - 45 minutes, until solid in center, then either serve hot or cold, good either way.

As a side note, this recipe can be modified tremendously, although this is my personal favorite. Another good variation is using just a pound of mushrooms and swiss cheese. Diced peppers can be used too.
