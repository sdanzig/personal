Beaver Tails

Dough ingredients:

  • ½ c warm water (Warm means 80-90 F. Over 100 F might kill the yeast)
  • 5 ts yeast
  • pinch of white sugar
  • 1 c warm milk
  • ⅓ c sugar
  • 1½ ts salt
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla
  • 2 beaten eggs
  • ⅓ c canola oil
  • 5 c all-purpose flour

Cinnamon-sugar coating ingredients:

  • 2 c sugar
  • 3 ts cinnamon

Mix the warm water, yeast, and pinch of sugar in a large bowl. Let rise for 5 minutes.

Add all remaining dough ingredients except the flour and stir until smooth. Mix in the flour, then knead for 8 minutes.

Place in a lightly greased bowl, cover with a damp (moistened, not dripping wet) cloth towel, and let rise for 35 minutes.

Put the 2 cups sugar and the cinnamon in a closed container and shake vigorously to mix.

Gently punch down the inflated dough then pinch off small balls of dough and roll into ovals. Place balls aside, covered with cloth towel and let rest while you finish the rest of the balls. Heat 4 inches of oil in a frying pan, to 375 degrees. Just before cooking, stretch ball into a beaver tail shape, flattening or enlarging as needed. Fry in oil, turning only once, until deep brown. Drain off oil then quickly toss in cinnamon sugar while still hot.

Notes:
Best served warm and can be reheated in a microwave.
This dough can be made in a bread machine on the dough setting by placing all dough ingredients except flour and yeast in pan, completely covering with flour, then sprinkling yeast on top. Once the dough cycle is complete, place the mixture to rise as described above.
