Coq au Vin

  • ½ c all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp pepper
  • 3 to 3½ pound cut-up broiler-fryer chicken. If you're not sure how to cut up the chicken, just cut the thing down the middle with a pair of kitchen scissors, then remove the legs and a bit of meat with the wings and you'll get something close enough from there.
  • 8 slices bacon
  • ¾ c frozen small whole onions (from what usually is a 16 oz bag)
  • 3 c sliced mushrooms (8 oz)
  • 1 c chicken broth
  • 1 c dry red wine
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 4 medium (6-7 inch) carrots, cut into 2 inch pieces
  • 1 clove garlic, finely chopped
  • a bouquet garni, which can be made by taking 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme leaves, 2 large sprigs fresh parsley, and 1 bay leaf, wrapping it all in cheesecloth and tying it into a bag using a bit of twine. Make sure to appreciate the smell of the bay leaf :)

Mix flour, 1 teaspoon salt and the pepper. Coat chicken parts with flour mixture.

Cook bacon in 12-inch skillet over medium heat until crisp. Remove bacon with slotted spoon and then lay on paper towel, patting them down with another sheet. Set the bacon aside and cook the chicken in the bacon fat over medium heat flipping the pieces over with a spatula every couple of minutes, until the chicken is browned on all sides, then give it a minute or two more. (Approximately at least 15 minutes)

Move chicken to one side of the skillet and add the onions and mushrooms to the other side.  Cook over medium-high heat, stirring generously, until the mushrooms are tender. Tilt the skillet over the sink to drain the fat.

Crumble the bacon and stir it with the rest of the ingredients into the vegetables, making sure the bouquet garni is as deep in the sauce as possible. Heat to boiling then reduce heat to low. Cover the pan and simmer for about 35 minutes or until the juice of the chicken is no longer pink when the centers of the thickest pieces are cut. Remove bouquet garni.  Skim off excess fat.
