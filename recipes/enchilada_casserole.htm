Enchilada Casserole

  • 1½ lb lean ground beef
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 1 (8 oz) can tomato sauce
  • 1 (12 oz) can Mexicorn, drained (usually corn mixed with green and red peppers and maybe other things)
  • 1 (2¼ oz) can sliced ripe olives, drained
  • 1 (10 oz) can hot enchilada sauce. Careful not to accidentally buy mild. That would ruin the dish in my eyes.
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp oregano
  • ¼ tsp chili powder
  • ¼ tsp pepper
  • 12 small corn tortillas. They have a diameter of about 6 inches. If you can't find corn tortillas, flour tortillas work too.
  • 2 cups shredded cheese, preferably a mix of cheddar and Monterey, but definitely at least cheddar.

In 2-quart casserole, combine ground beef and onion. Cover. Microwave at high for 6 to 10 minutes until meat is thoroughly cooked; stir after 5 minutes. You can assume that all microwave cooking is done uncovered, except the reheating at the end.  Drain meat thoroughly, squeezing the juice out of the beef and patting it down afterwards with paper towel. Add tomato sauce, corn, ripe olives, enchilada sauce, salt, oregano, chili powder and pepper. Microwave at high for 3 to 5 minutes.

Place six of the tortillas around the bottom of a 2-quart oblong baking dish. (13x9 in.) Try not to overlap the tortillas much, just overlapping the edges, spreading them in an oval, fully covering the bottom of the baking dish and part of the sides as well. Pour half of the meat mixture over the tortillas. Sprinkle 1 cup of the cheese on top. Repeat the layers, starting with another layer of tortillas laid out the same way, then the rest of the meat mixture, then the cheese. Microwave at high for 10 minutes, or until the cheese is fully melted, or a few more minutes if you want to serve the dish right away. For a stiffer casserole (improves the presentation a bit and makes serving easier), refrigerate overnight and to serve, microwave, covered with vent holes, to preferred heat.
