Raisin Scones

  • 1¾ c all-purpose flour
  • 3 tbsp sugar
  • 2½ tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • ⅓ c firm butter
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • ½ c raisins
  • 6 tbsp heavy cream, plus additional for coating
  • white coarse sugar crystals (decorating sugar)

Heat oven to 400 F.

Mix flour, 3 tablespoons sugar, baking powder and salt in large bowl. Cut in butter by crisscrossing 2 knives until mixture looks like fine crumbs. Stir in egg, raisins and just enough of the 6 tablespoons heavy cream so dough leaves side of bowl.

Place dough on lightly floured surface; gently roll in flour to coat. Knead lightly 10 times. Roll or pat into 8 inch circle on ungreased cookie sheet. Cut into 8 wedges with sharp knife that has been dipped in flour, but do not separate wedges. Brush with additional cream; sprinkle with sugar crystals.

Bake 15 minutes or until golden. Immediately remove from cookie sheet. Carefully separate wedges. Very good served warm.
