Fudgiest Brownies

  • ¾ c flour
  • ¼ tsp baking powder (not baking soda!)
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 3 oz unsweetened chocolate
  • ½ c unsalted butter (1 stick)
  • 1 c super-fine/caster/castor sugar (coarser than confectioner's/10x sugar, but finer than standard granulated sugar)
  • 1 egg
  • 2 egg yolks.  Try not to break the yolks until you're actually mixing them.  It lets you pour the eggs from a cup more easily.
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • ½ c chopped pecans
  • 6 oz semi-sweet or white chocolate chopped into ¼ to ½ inch pieces (Ghirardelli makes thin baking bars good for hand-chopping)

Keep the 6 oz chopped chocolate in a cup in the freezer for at least half an hour, and don't take it out until actually mixing it in.

Grease an 8 by 8 inch baking pan very lightly with vegetable shortening. Preheat oven to 350 degrees.

Mix flour, baking powder, and salt thoroughly in a small bowl. Set aside.

Melt chocolate and butter in a large saucepan over very low heat, stirring just until there are no more lumps. Remove from heat immediately.

Beat in the super-fine sugar, the egg, and the yolks until well-blended.  Stir in the flour mixture just until blended.  Stir in vanilla and nuts.

Remove the frozen chocolate chips and quickly stir in, then quickly spread the whole mixture into the 8x8 pan.

Bake for 19 minutes. Cool in pan on wire rack.
