Turtle Brownies

  • 4 squares unsweetened baking chocolate
  • ¾ c margarine
  • 2 c sugar
  • 4 eggs
  • 1 c flour
  • 1 pkg. (14 oz.) caramels, unwrapped - Kraft caramels are easy nicely packaged and easy to unwrap, but you can probably find a cheaper brand.
  • ⅓ c heavy cream
  • 2 c pecans, divided
  • 12 oz. of semi sweet chocolate baking bars, cut into ¾ inch squares.

Heat chocolate squares and margarine in a medium sauce pan on very low heat, stirring constantly until completely melted. Stir sugar into chocolate, mix in eggs, and then stir in flour just until fully mixed in. (Mixing extra decreases the density of the brownies.)  Spread at least ½ of batter in a greased (melted butter/margarine, vegetable shortening, or Pam cooking spray is fine) 13" x 9" baking pan, reserving the rest for the top layer. Bake at 350°F until it solidifies a bit, so you can spread other layers over it, but do not let it start to darken.  The original recipe called for 25 minutes, but I find that it would get blackened long before that.

In a small sauce pan, over low heat, stir caramels and cream constantly until completely melted. Initially, you won't be able to easily stir a pile of sticky caramel blocks, but just get it to budge slightly back and forth and eventually it will melt enough for you to stir all the way around. Next, stir in 1 cup of pecans. Gently spread caramel mixture over brownie batter in pan, almost all the way to the edges.  Don't worry if it does reach the edges, but note that the caramel tends to seep over a bit leaving a more chewy edge.

Sprinkle evenly with chocolate chunks. Pour remaining unbaked brownie batter evenly over caramel mixture and sprinkle with remaining pecans.

Bake an additional 30 minutes, checking every couple of minutes after 20.  If you start to see the edges darken, it's probably ready and about to get overcooked.  Cool in pan and run knife along edge of pan to loosen brownies out.
