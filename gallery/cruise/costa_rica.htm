Visiting Costa Rica

 

Here's a typical house behind a typical palm tree not far from the Caribbean coast of Costa Rica.

 

I think we were passing over a very skinny bridge and I took a picture at the scenery below, but it doesn't look as impressive in this picture.

 

This is another picture from the bus.  Costa Rica enjoys lots of coastal flooding, and you can see the aftermath beyond the handful of trees.

 

After biking to the beach in Costa Rica, we reached the beach. Stupid me still doesn't own a pair of sandals, so I walked out there doing the "hot sand dance".  Let me be clear about something though. This beach looked MUCH more impressive in person.  The water here looks murky blue, but in person, it was perfectly clear turquoise water. I can describe it like greenish gefilte fish gel, but that would sound disgusting.  The water was very salty though, and I learned that because of all the salt, it required no effort to stay afloat.  We did a decent lot of swimming, and some people vacationing in the area had boats like shown.  I saw one guy throw a stick into the water for his dog to fetch.  It was too cute to watch with a straight face.

 

Here's another picture of the beach, in the other direction... to the west I think. One more thing to note is that I felt very itchy later in the day after swimming in this water, and someone else I talked to felt the same.

 

We stopped by one hotel for coffee and fruit.  Not much of a meal, but the mango was good.  They also had a pathetically small jacuzzi.  The rooms of this hotel were a bunch of bungalows on stilts scattered throughout the trees.  The idea was that they wanted it to blend in well, and they certainly achieved that.  Anyway, what you see here is a pair of beds in one of the bungalows.  I bet  you didn't guess that.

 

And here's a nice hammock you can rest in, obviously. I forget what that is behind it to the left, but I'll guess it's a lamp. The bungalows have electric power.

 

They also have plumbing!

 

Here's an outside view of one of the bungalows.  I suppose it would have made more sense to show you this picture first, but I didn't want to bother rearranging them.
