Scott received his Master's Degree in Electrical Engineering from Columbia University on May 20th, 2003

 

 

Here's me in my cap and gown, about to leave the house for New York.  I'm in my parents' driveway, if you couldn't tell.  Look at how nicely I have ironed my gown.  It was very wrinkly at first.  Well, the instructions for ironing the gown says to turn it inside out first, which I did.   I'm not sure exactly why, but I think there were dark spots that formed on the outside of the gown after I ironed it (none on the inside).  I think the heat might have been too high, but anyway, I was nervous about the dark spots.  A new gown costs $292, and $46 just to rent.  I thought because of my little mistake, I might have been out three hundred dollars.  So at the time of this picture, I was worried my mother would notice the spots.  She didn't.  Later I found little things wrong with the gown that I was certain I didn't cause, so I figured it was no big deal returning the gown as is, being that it was still wearable and I wasn't even sure if I damaged the gown at all.

 

Here's my parents wanting to pose before we left.  My mother is very happy about this picture.  She likes the fact that she showed up pretty well, and my dad didn't make a goofy face for once.  Might I note that I think my mother has a nice outfit on.

 

Here's me standing in front of the most famous building at Columbia University's campus, the "Low Library".  The engineers who designed the library did not account for the weight of the books, and so the building started to sink when they were added.  They had to build another library, and to this day, use the Low Library as a monstrous administrative building.

 

Here's me and my brother, Rob.  Keep in mind, the sun is rather bright, so that's why we're somewhat squinting in so many of these pictures.  I'm just happy my mother aimed the camera right.

 

Here's my and my dad in this pose set up by my mother who was still trying to figure out my camera.  My dad looks a bit too serious here.  His stylish hat made him very easy to find in the crowd, let me add.

 

This is a view of the friends and family of the Columbia engineering graduates, seated, watching the "Engineering Class Day" ceremony.  You can see one of the Columbia University campus buildings towering overhead.

 

This shows more buildings surrounding the ceremony, which took place at the "South Lawn", which is a grassy field between the Low Library and the Butler Library which sits in front of and downhill from it.

 

Yes, my mother held the camera perfectly straight here.  I think the angled shot looks cool anyway.  This is, of course, another building on campus.  The blue banners hung along the building wall were I believe decorative for the ceremony.  There were four of them.  You can see some of the people watching the ceremony at the bottom.  Strangely, two of them are covering their faces with paper.  It was hot out so they might have also been fanning themselves, but there was another picture taken not long after this one which I tossed which showed the sheets of paper in the same exact position

 

This is a humorous story.  So I'm walking up towards the stage to shake hands with the dean.  My mother wants to take a picture of me on the way, with my digital camera.  I think she pressed a stray button or something, because she tried about 4 times and the camera never beeped (which it does when a picture is taken).  I got really pissed, because I was posing for each of her attempts to take a picture, all while being shoved along by the other graduates.  I eventually came up with an idea of what went wrong and yelled it to her.  She was able to fix the camera and take a picture in time to catch me as shown here.  For your convenience, I'm pointed out by the red arrow.

 

This is another picture of the students walking across the stage to shake hands with the dean, who is the red splotch to the left of the center.  The walk was horribly screwed up when the cards with the names on them were out of order.  Students sometimes were rushing across the stage, getting their picture taken by a photographer with a trigger finger faster than Billy the Kid's.  The black splotch to the right of the dean was actually another guy who hosted the ceremony who I didn't know but wanted to shake my hand anyway.  They called my name a bit fast and I felt I had to hurry to get on stage, but I made sure I gave the photographer plenty of time to get a decent shot of me shaking the dean's hand.  They also gave us a gold pin in a little case (instead of the diploma) which supposed to look like gold but probably is a cheap, electroplated metal.  Maybe I'll show a picture of it here when I have a camera that can handle it.

 

Lastly, here's a picture of the seating area after the ceremony.  Beyond all the empty chairs, you can see a bright white tent and take my word for it that there are lots of people in and all around it.  They're all scavenging for the free food during the reception, which consisted of cookies and crackers, cheeses, fruit, snack vegetables, cups of fruit cocktail, and some drinks, which due to the hot weather, were in very high demand.
