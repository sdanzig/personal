Scott Proposes to Aliona on Valentine's Weekend, February 11th, 2012




It took all the nudging in the world, but I finally convinced Aliona that it was important to be in front of our building at precisely noon.  The stretch limo was waiting for us, and we hopped in.



It was my first time in a stretch limo too!  The little colorful specks of light, "fiber optic lights", change color all the time.



We arrived at Bryant Park Grill.  I've been there once before and knew the food was classy and healthy.  I knew it would be perfect for Aliona!  She was in the mood for mimosas.  I was in the mood for a peach bellini.



Our food had arrived.  I did like my lobster roll, but Aliona was ecstatic about her potato pancake.



My months of planning this weekend was all paying off.



Here's a closer look at Aliona's salmon dish, topped with the potato pancake and a wine sauce.



We got back into the limo and arrived at the Mandarin Oriental hotel on the other (west) side of Central Park, at Columbus Circle.  Here's our view.



I wanted to let her escape from her hectic student life in a serene, stylish setting.  My jacket usually not this reflective, but the camera's flash makes it seem so.



The hotel left us complimentary chocolate covered strawberries on a bed of white chocolate shavings.  I had already eaten two of them.



I had the hotel have flowers waiting for us, which we ended up taking home.  At the time of this writing, we are protecting them from the jaws of kitten Luna.



There was a perfectly placed lounge chair alongside our room's window which quickly became Aliona's favorite spot in the room.



Upon nightfall, we were immersed in the sparkling city lights.



This picture is after a lot happened.  To sum it up, I brought Aliona to Masa, one of the world's most expensive restaurants, and during 27 courses of Japanese goodies, I had proposed to her with a 6 line speech that I had run through my head twenty times earlier that day.  Thankfully I remembered the right knee, and the Tiffany ring fit her perfectly.  Everyone applauded and the restaurant gave us a complimentary dessert.  Unfortunately, the shock killed Aliona's appetite.



Here's a picture of the ring, by the way.



It was a multilayer cake made of green tea flavored crepe.  We thought the sake bottle candle was cute.



The staff took our picture behind the sushi bar.



I had went around New York looking for the perfect bottle of Rose champagne and had the hotel have that and berries waiting for us upon our return to the room.



Yes, I also went for the rose petals!  Aliona thought the perfectly aligned petals on the shelf was a bit OCD but was nonetheless swept off her feet!



Aliona did not want to leave her favorite chair, but she wanted more berries.



I passed her the bowl.



It was time for more champagne!



The camera was perfectly balanced on a champagne glass as I ran over to be photographed for this magical moment.  I knew it was magical because I did not trip.



It seemed destiny to mix the berries and champagne.  We thought the strawberry looked a bit like Pacman, but I was also reminded of Little Shop of Horrors.



The view in the morning was dreamy, with lazy clouds in the sky.



No matter what Aliona and I were up to, Columbus Circle was always busy.  Every so often, a horse and carraige would trot through and traffic would slow to a crawl.



Thanks to CNN, we didn't need watches.



I dragged Aliona to a prix fixe brunch at the hotel the next morning.  We didn't have much choice, so I was forced to eat this poultry for breakfast.



Aliona thought so much fancy food was getting a bit silly.



And on that note, more salmon and duck was served.



At this point, I was the only one still eating this chocolate raspberry mousse cake and the best creme brulee ever, complete with almond cookie.



We finished up in plenty time to make it downtown for the helicopter tour I reserved for us.  It was meant to be a surprise, but Aliona spied the word "helicopter" on some of the paperwork I was sifting through.



Statue of Liberty waved hi to us.



Here's our pilot.  We had headphones and mics, but it's awkward to talk on them.  The pilot tried to make conversation with us, but we felt too awkward to say much back.  Must be lonely for him, but he gets to fly over New York all day without fighter pilots shooting at him, so not too bad.



Look Mom, I'm in a helicopter.  The last time I showed her a picture like this, she got all nervous, thinking I joined the military.



The flight was very fun, but at this point, we started worrying about nausea.  The pilot was taking liberties with the how far the helicopter would swing to the side when we turned.  Here we flew over the Cyclone at the Coney Island amusement park.



Boats are wrapped up during the icy winter.



Best picture ever of my favorite bridge, the Verrazano.



We saw a very freaky set of art exhibits on Governor's Island, shown here, including one with hundreds of carefully photographed traffic cones.



Here's downtown New York, where most of the world is profited off of.



The World Trade Center reconstruction is looking good.  Both Aliona and I missed out on the opportunity to see the twin towers, so we'll be sure to fully appreciate this one.



The Empire State Building is right near the eye doctor in Koreatown that Aliona and I go to.



Here's the piers around midtown west, including the Intrepid Sea, Air and Space museum.



Flying northbound, we see the northern half of Central Park, with some of Queens behind it.



The pilot made such a big deal of Skankee Stadium.  Okay, I'll take just one picture, but that's it.



As we swerved away from the stadium, these bridges lined up on what I think is the Harlem River, so my camera took a picture.



Aliona took this delightful picture of the George Washington Bridge.



The tourists sitting across from us took this picture for us.  We were taking a lot of pictures for each other.  It was a bonding experience.



My last surprise was again not really a surprise, as Aliona had spent all day interrogating me, finding out subtle details until she figured out we were going on a dinner cruise.  The Empire State Building was lit blue and white.  It was a sign from my mother who wants a proper Jewish wedding.



With the platinum romance package, we had lovely window seats, roses, and more champagne waiting for us at our table.  Aliona used the champagne to make more mimosas.



They announced our engagement to everyone on the cruise, and we got a bunch of "oooos" and applause.



Here's a dimly lit shot at the others onboard, mostly celebrating anniversaries and birthdays.



There was live music with some people dancing nearby.



It was pretty cold and windy outside, but we still wanted to go out and take pictures.



Aliona spent the remainder of her stay outside putting on more clothes.  We were frolicking about and giggling, then decided to finally go back in, just as the captain was rushing out, probably to scold us for being silly.  Since we were done, he sheepishly turned right back around and said nothing.



More fancy food.  We made sure to starve ourselves most of the day so we could physically consume another prix fixe meal.  During this cruise, the realization that I had gone overboard with the fancy food did not set in.  It probably hit home the night before, actually.  I think I had some form of herb-roasted chicken while Aliona had a salmon dish.



We floated by the Statue of Liberty and me, along with everyone else, rushed out to take this stunning picture.



A photographer went around taking pictures of the couples on board and showing them the photos, for sale, on an iPad.  We didn't like our photos too much, so we had our waitress take this one for free.



The singer visited our side of the boat near the end of the cruise.  Afterward, we raced off the ship and tried desperately to find a cab outside Chelsea Piers.  It turns out, it's very easy to find one if you walk one avenue in, but we didn't figure that out until frostbite set in.  Aliona slept well that night and spent the next day telling her overjoyed parents the news, then tried desperately to get back to studying for an exam.
