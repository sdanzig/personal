Recipes

Ever since I first moved into an apartment and had my own kitchen, cooking became a frequent hobby of mine. It provided good stress relief after a hard day of computer programming.  I have an obsession for good food, especially French and German, and I have plenty of experience with desserts.  Anyway, I seem to have gotten rave reviews over the years from family, friends and coworkers, so I figured I'd provide my versions of the most successful recipes on my web page. Feel free to email me your favorites as well and I'll add them. I also can add recipes upon request.

Entrees

  • Coq au Vin - Mixture of chicken, bacon, vegetables, in wine, flavored with a French blend of herbs.
  • Djej Matisha Mesla - Chicken in a sweet cinnamon tomato sauce. This Moroccan recipe is from a friend in Holland.
  • Enchilada Casserole - Hearty, spicy, familiar-tasting Tex-Mex-style dish consisting of tortillas, beef, hot enchilada sauce and an assortment of nicely complementary ingredients.
  • Indonesian Peanut Chicken - Originating from southeast Asia. A thick and textured peanut sauce coats chicken and blends with rice underneath.
  • Lamb-Haricots - French dish of tender lamb immersed in beans, vegetables, white wine, and chicken stock.
  • Meat Loaf - A version of meat loaf that tastes irresistible.
  • Schwalbennester - A German dish.  Veal wrapped around eggs, in a wine sauce.
Side dishes
  • Bazin-quiche - Named after a good friend who supplied this quiche recipe; here is my hearty variation of it.
  • Blueberry Crunch Cake - A light, southern-style corn bread-textured cake that is excellent with fried chicken.
  • Raisin Scones - Buttery English biscuit, great with breakfast or tea time.
  • Southern Buttermilk Corn Bread - Warm corn bread is great with any meal, but you'll find this even better.
Desserts
  • Almond Crunch Pumpkin Cheesecake - Pumpkin-flavored cheesecake covered in a layer of toasted almonds, in a graham cracker crust.
  • Banana-Nut Bread - Great dessert for any time of the day, and everyone likes it. Also very hard to mess up this recipe.
  • Banana-White Chocolate Chip Biscotti - Delicious cookies that complements a coffee break perfectly.
  • Beaver Tails - Canadian cinnamon doughnut-like dessert that resembles a beaver's tail.
  • Blueberry Sour Cream Coffee Cake - Simple recipe from a Canadian cookbook: nuts and cinnamon separate two layers of moist, rich coffee cake spotted with blueberries.
  • Ricotta Cheese Pie - A ricotta-based cheesecake-like dessert that's not too sweet but extremely smooth. Beautiful presentation, and great served hot or cold.
  • Fudgiest Brownies - Super-dense brownies with chocolate chips and nuts, topped with an impressive delicate, crispy, shiny layer on top.
  • Indian Pudding - Thanksgiving-style dessert of the English colonists, has the texture of pumpkin pie, and goes great with ice cream.
  • Lemon-Coconut Bars - Chewy lemon-coconut mixture over a buttery crust.
  • Rice Pudding - Old-fashioned rice pudding made thick enough to be served in squares. Wonderful taste and very filling.
  • Tiramisu - An elegant Italian dessert with a mocha java flavor and a mousse-like texture.
  • Turtle Brownies - Brownies with a caramel-pecan-chocolate chip layer, topped with pecans.
